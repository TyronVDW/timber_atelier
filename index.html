The Timber Atelier premise

Furniture should not expire.

We do not manufacture. We make one commission at a time—in solid timber, with joinery that earns its place and finishes that let the grain speak.

No catalogue. No repeats. The result is a piece that belongs to its room, its material and the person who asked for it.

The timber library

Chosen board by board.

We do not prescribe a species before we understand the piece. Grain, movement, touch and the life it will live all matter.

01

Kiaat

Southern Africa Warm, open grain that deepens to amber-brown. +

Beautifully workable and naturally composed, kiaat is at home in dining tables, doors and pieces made for coastal living. Best for: tables, doors, outdoor pieces.

02

Yellowwood

South Africa · Reclaimed & licensed stock Pale gold, fine and quietly luminous. +

The timber of Cape heirlooms has a calm, refined figure. We use reclaimed and licensed stock only. Best for: heirloom tables, panelling, restoration.

03

Imbuia

The KwaZulu-Natal favourite Smoky chocolate tones with expressive figure. +

A classic of Natal furniture-making, rich without becoming loud and exceptionally suited to rooms with presence. Best for: boardroom tables, studies, statement pieces.

04

French oak

Europe Honest, structural and endlessly versatile. +

French oak takes texture, fuming and liming beautifully while retaining a clear architectural grain. Best for: doors, panelling, framed construction.

05

Walnut, rosewood, iroko & ash

Selected for the commission A wider palette for colour, curve and climate. +

From black walnut’s quiet depth to iroko’s coastal resilience and ash’s ability to bend, the material follows the work—not fashion. We also mill meaningful client-supplied trees where the timber is suitable.

Species are sourced with their paperwork in order—licensed, plantation-grown or reclaimed. Availability changes, as it should with a natural material.

How a commission unfolds

From first line to final placement.

You remain close to the work. The drawings are clear, the quotation is fixed, and the piece is photographed as it grows.

The conversation

We meet at the atelier or your site and talk about the room, the use and the life the piece will live.

Week zero

Drawings & timber

Scaled drawings, a fixed quotation and a walk through the available timber to choose your boards.

Weeks 1–3

The build

One bench, one maker and regular progress photographs. You are welcome to visit mid-build.

Weeks 4–12

Delivery & placement

We deliver, place and finish on site, then return after a season to check how it has settled.

The atelier · Ballito

One bench. One maker. One piece at a time.

Minutes from Ballito’s beaches, the atelier is part bench room, part timber store and part drawing office. Most commissions begin with coffee here and a conversation over the boards.

We take a limited number of projects so each one receives the drawing time, bench time and finishing time it deserves.
